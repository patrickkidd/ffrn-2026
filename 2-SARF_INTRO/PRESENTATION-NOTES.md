## III. The SARF Model: Four Variables (28 min)

**The pipeline:** R → A → S. F is the lever.

**S — Symptom**
- Whatever brings them in. Track: up, down, same.

**A — Anxiety**
- Not "feeling stressed." Perceived uncertainty + urgency.
- Anxious search: grabs anything that reduces uncertainty, stops whether problem is solved or not
- Thinking degrades as anxiety rises
- Track: up, down, same

**R — Relationship**
- Automatic moves toward/away: conflict, distance, over/under-functioning, child-focus, cutoff, triangle
- Define self = sophisticated option, requires higher F
- "Automatic" = evolutionarily older behavioral model, not chosen

**F — Functioning**
- Emotion/thinking balance toward goals (goals defined at calmest)
- Clinical goal: "function up"
- Higher F buffers impact of R and A on S

## IV. Back to the Case: SARF Applied (5 min)

## V. The Timeline Method (4 min)

- You already do intakes — this is about what you're looking for
- Four steps: when did S start → what R shifts → plot on timeline → rule out A and R
- Takes a handful of sessions with a trained person
- Client buy-in may be mandatory — they need to understand the approach from the start
- Anxiety impairs reporting accuracy — skilled interviewing required
