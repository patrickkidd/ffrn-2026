The nursing trainees want to treat the pain—bandage the fingers, maybe anxiety meds.

## THE SARF MODEL: Four Variables (28 min)

### The Pipeline: R → A → S

**Relationship shifts** drive **Anxiety** which drives **Symptoms**. **Functioning** is the lever.

Instead of asking "what's wrong with this person?", ask "what's happening in their relationships, and how is that showing up as symptoms?"

### S: Symptom

Whatever brings them in. Mental health, behavioral, emotional, physical.

- Track: up, down, same

### A: Anxiety

**Not** "feeling stressed." **Anxiety = perceived uncertainty + urgency.**

- When anxious, people search for *anything* to reduce uncertainty
- Thinking degrades as anxiety rises—they jump topics, contradict themselves, can't answer direct questions
- The search stops when a "solution" is found—whether the problem is actually solved or not
- Track: up, down, same

### R: Relationship

The *automatic* moves people make toward/away from each other:

- **Conflict** – fighting, overt tension
- **Distance** – withdrawal, avoidance
- **Over/under-functioning** – one does more, one does less
- **Child-focus** – anxiety channeled into worry about a kid
- **Cutoff** – severing contact
- **Triangle** – pulling in a third party to stabilize

And the sophisticated option:

- **Define self** – taking a position while staying connected (requires higher F)

"Automatic" here means driven by an evolutionarily older behavioral model. Not chosen. Not deliberate. Short-term motivated.

### F: Functioning

How well someone balances emotion and thinking toward their goals (goals defined at their calmest).

- Low F: ruled by emotion, can't plan, can't see forest for trees
- High F: can pause, think long-term, update their approach
- **Clinical goal: "function up"**
- F is the independent variable—higher F buffers the impact of R and A on S

## BACK TO THE CASE: SARF Applied (5 min)

**Presenting complaint:** Daughter bites nails. "Always been there."

| Variable | What I Found |
|----------|--------------|
| **S** (Symptom) | Nail-biting in daughter |
| **R** (Relationship) | Boyfriend fled → Mother's relationship system destabilized |
| **A** (Anxiety) | Mother's anxiety spiked (uncertainty + urgency) |
| **S** (Symptom) | Daughter's nail-biting *started* at the same time |

**The standard implicit model:** Problem is in the daughter. Locate it. Fix it.

**The SARF model:** R → A → S. The mother's relationship shift drove her anxiety, which showed up as symptoms in the daughter.

**Intervention:** Work on mother's **F** (functioning). Help her manage her own anxiety in relationships rather than channeling it into worry about her daughter.

**Outcome:** Two sessions. Symptom remission. Mother forgot it existed.

**The blade of grass:** The daughter's nail-biting was like a blade of grass bending in the wind. You can measure the grass all day—angle, position, structure—and still not predict when it moves. Because the grass isn't the phenomenon. *The wind is.* The wind was in the mother's relationship system.

## THE TIMELINE METHOD: How You Collect This (4 min)

You already do intakes. This is about what you're looking for.

1. **When did the symptom start?** Get a date, not a narrative.
2. **What was happening in relationships at that time?** Look for R shifts—conflict, distance, cutoff, triangle shifts, someone moving in or out.
3. **Plot S, A, and R on a timeline.** Do the shifts correlate?
4. **Rule out:** Can you rule out A and R as factors in S? If not, that's your clinical territory.

### The Reality

This usually takes a handful of sessions with a trained person. It's not a one-intake exercise.

It helps—might even be mandatory—for the client to be on board with the process from the start. If they don't understand why you're asking about relationships and timelines instead of diving straight into the symptom, the data collection stalls.

Anxiety impairs the very cognition needed to report accurately. Clients jump topics, contradict themselves, can't answer simple questions. Skilled interviewing is required to tease facts from anxious distortions.

## TOOLS: Where This Is Going (3 min)

### Family Diagram "Pro" App (released)

- SARF variables built in
- Timeline and family diagram visualization
- Designed for clinicians tracking cases over time

### "Personal" AI Intake App (in development)

- AI chatbot walks someone through their own timeline and family diagram
- Makes data collection accessible to anyone—not just trained clinicians
- Creates a case file that "Pro" can open
- Intake tool: client does groundwork before first session

### Inter-Rater Reliability Study (R&D)

- Can domain experts agree on SARF coding of the same case?
- If yes → train AI to auto-code
- This is the path from clinical observation to testable science

## CLOSE (2 min)

SARF gives us common variables. Common variables let us coordinate. Coordination lets us do real research. Real research could finally test whether relationships impact symptoms the way we think they do.

**Before your next intake:** Ask when the symptom started. Ask what was happening in relationships at that time. Track R, A, S, and F. See what you find.
