# The Refrigerator Mother Theory (1950s-1970s)

**Summary for:** FFRN Conference Presentation on Social Contagion in Helping Professions
**Original Sources:** [Embryo Project Encyclopedia (ASU)](https://embryo.asu.edu/pages/early-infantile-autism-and-refrigerator-mother-theory-1943-1970), [AMA Journal of Ethics](https://journalofethics.ama-assn.org/article/mothers-and-autism-evolution-discourse-blame/2015-04), [Quackwatch](https://quackwatch.org/autism/causes/rm/), [The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/from-refrigerator-mothers-to-paracetamol-why-harmful-autism-myths-are-so-common-266075)

## Overview

The refrigerator mother theory was a discredited psychological theory claiming that autism was caused by a lack of maternal emotional warmth. For approximately 20 years (1950s-1970s), mothers were blamed for causing their children's neurological condition, leading to immense guilt, shame, and the forced separation of children from families.

---

## Origins: Leo Kanner

### The First Description of Autism (1943)
- Leo Kanner, Austrian-American psychiatrist, published first clinical description of autism
- Characterized it as "early infantile autism"—unique disorder marked by social withdrawal, repetitive behaviors, language difficulties
- In his 1943 paper, Kanner noted what he perceived as a lack of parental warmth

### The Shift to Blame (1949)
- Kanner's 1949 paper: attributed autism to "genuine lack of maternal warmth"
- Coined the term "refrigerator mother"
- **1960 interview:** Kanner described parents of autistic children as "just happening to defrost enough to produce a child"

---

## Bruno Bettelheim: The Amplifier

### Background
- University of Chicago professor and child development specialist
- Director of Sonia Shankman Orthogenic School (residential treatment facility)
- Built reputation as leading specialist in autism treatment

### His Claims
- Autism was an **emotional disorder** caused by psychological harm from mothers
- Biological abnormalities were **effects, not causes** of autism
- Compared autistic children's withdrawal to concentration camp prisoners (Bettelheim was a Holocaust survivor)

### His Influence
- 1967: Published *The Empty Fortress: Infantile Autism and the Birth of the Self*
- Advocated **"parent-ectomies"**—removing autistic children from parents' care
- Articles in 1950s-60s gave theory widespread popularity

### Later Revelations (1990)
- Upon Bettelheim's death, discovered he had:
  - **Lied about his credentials**
  - **Abused patients** at his facility
- His "expertise" was largely fabricated

---

## How the Theory Spread

1. **Psychoanalytic dominance:** Theory fit prevailing Freudian framework
2. **Apparent logic:** Children seemed withdrawn; mothers seemed reserved
3. **Expert endorsement:** Kanner and Bettelheim were leading authorities
4. **No competing explanation:** Biological basis not yet understood
5. **Guilt as confirmation:** Mothers who felt guilty were seen as proving the theory

---

## Harm Caused

### To Mothers
- Blamed for children's neurological condition
- Intense guilt and shame
- Many reported "deep sense of anguish and resentment toward child psychiatrists"
- Reinforced harmful gender stereotypes about parenting

### To Families
- Children **institutionalized** because "the mother was the problem"
- Families torn apart
- Siblings affected by family dynamics
- "Her brother was eventually institutionalized, in part because federal legal protections did not exist at the time to ensure his right to an education"

### To Treatment
- Focus on "fixing" mothers rather than helping children
- Delayed development of effective interventions
- Resources wasted on psychoanalytic approaches

---

## The Debunking

### Bernard Rimland (1964)
- Psychologist and **father of an autistic son**
- Published *Infantile Autism: The Syndrome and its Implications for a Neural Theory of Behavior*
- Attacked refrigerator mother hypothesis directly
- Identified Kanner's reasoning as **post hoc ergo propter hoc fallacy** (inferring cause from correlation without evidence)
- Proposed biological mechanisms, citing brain research

### Rimland's Advocacy
- 1965: Helped establish National Society for Autistic Children (NSAC)
- 1967: Co-founded Institute for Child Behavioral Research
- Provided support and information for parents

### Kanner's Reversal (1969)
- At NSAC meeting in Washington D.C.
- Stated he "acquitted the parents"
- Insisted autism was innate
- **Despite this, blame continued** in psychiatry clinics across the country

### Scientific Evidence (Late 1970s+)
- Twin studies suggested genetic etiology
- Non-social environmental factors identified
- Modern research: largely **epigenetic etiology** of autism spectrum disorders
